## RECITALS

WHEREAS, Vendor has developed the Emergency Response Offline SDK, a mobile software development kit designed to assist outdoor recreationists in transmitting emergency location data when cellular voice service is unavailable ("the System");

WHEREAS, Agency desires to evaluate the System through a time-limited pilot to assess its potential as a supplemental emergency communication tool for Agency-managed public lands, waterways, or programs;

WHEREAS, both parties agree that this pilot is exploratory in nature and does not constitute a commitment to procure, deploy, or endorse the System;

NOW, THEREFORE, the parties agree as follows:

---

## ARTICLE 1 — SCOPE OF PILOT

**1.1 Pilot Purpose.** The pilot is intended solely to evaluate the technical capabilities, operational utility, and integration feasibility of the System. No real-world emergency response decisions should be made solely on the basis of data transmitted through the System during this pilot without independent verification.

**1.2 Pilot Duration.** The pilot shall commence on [START DATE] and conclude on [END DATE], unless extended by mutual written agreement or terminated earlier under Article 8.

**1.3 Pilot Environment.** The pilot will be conducted in a demonstration/staging environment. No production 911 or CAD integration is active during the pilot. Agency acknowledges that the System operates independently of the state's public safety infrastructure.

**1.4 Supplemental Tool.** Both parties acknowledge that the System is a supplemental tool only. It is not a replacement for 911, E911, CAD-integrated dispatch, or any existing emergency communication protocol. Agency shall ensure this is communicated to all pilot participants and operators.

---

## ARTICLE 2 — RESPONSIBILITIES

**2.1 Vendor Responsibilities.**
- Provide access to the System, dashboard, and technical documentation
- Provide up to [X] hours of technical onboarding and support during the pilot period
- Maintain the demonstration server with reasonable best-effort availability (no SLA during pilot)

## ARTICLE 3 — LIMITATIONS AND NO WARRANTY

**3.1 Pilot Status.** The System is provided in its current pilot state, "as is," without warranty of any kind, including implied warranties of merchantability or fitness for a particular purpose.

**3.2 No Uptime Guarantee.** Vendor does not guarantee system availability, transmission success rates, or any specific response time during the pilot.

**3.3 No Emergency Response Guarantee.** This System does not guarantee emergency response. Agency acknowledges that the System is a supplemental digital reporting tool, not a certified emergency communications system.

---

## ARTICLE 4 — DATA OWNERSHIP AND USE

**4.1 Agency Data Ownership.** All incident data, GPS records, and user-submitted information transmitted through the System during the pilot belongs to Agency and its users. Vendor has no ownership claim to this data.

**4.2 Vendor Data Use.** Vendor may access pilot data only for the purpose of technical debugging, system improvement, and pilot evaluation. Vendor shall not use pilot data for commercial purposes, marketing, or disclosure to third parties.

**4.3 Anonymized Aggregate Data.** Vendor may retain anonymized, aggregated statistics (e.g., total packets transmitted, average GPS accuracy, network types observed) following pilot conclusion, stripped of all personally identifiable and agency-identifying information, for product improvement purposes, unless Agency objects in writing.

**4.4 Data Return and Destruction.** Upon pilot conclusion or Agreement termination, Vendor will either return or certifiably destroy all Agency data within 30 days, per Agency's written instruction.

**4.5 Data Breach Notification.** Vendor will notify Agency within 72 hours of discovering any unauthorized access to pilot data, consistent with Michigan's Identity Theft Protection Act (MCL 445.61 et seq.).

---

## ARTICLE 5 — CONFIDENTIALITY

**5.1 Mutual Confidentiality.** Each party agrees to keep confidential the other party's non-public technical, operational, and business information disclosed during the pilot, and not to disclose such information to third parties without prior written consent.

**5.2 Exceptions.** Confidentiality obligations do not apply to information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available without breach of this Agreement; (b) was known to the receiving party before disclosure; (c) is required to be disclosed by law or court order (with prompt written notice to the disclosing party).

**5.3 Public Records.** Agency acknowledges that as a Michigan state agency, certain records may be subject to disclosure under the Michigan Freedom of Information Act (MCL 15.231 et seq.).

## ARTICLE 6 — INDEMNIFICATION

**6.1 By Vendor.** Vendor shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Agency and its officers, employees, and agents from claims arising from Vendor's gross negligence or willful misconduct in connection with the System.

**6.2 By Agency.** Agency shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Vendor from claims arising from Agency's negligent or unauthorized deployment, modification, or use of the System.

**6.3 No Indemnity for Emergency Non-Response.** Neither party indemnifies the other for claims arising from failure of emergency response due to the inherent limitations of supplemental digital tools. Users are notified that 911 is the primary emergency resource.
